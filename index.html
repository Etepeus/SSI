THE SCIENCE BEHIND SSI

Where biology, energy and soil intelligence connect

SSI Technology is informed by a deeper understanding of soil as a living biological network. Developed through Norman Olsen’s long-term work in soil microbiology and biological soil systems, SSI is built around the principle that soil intelligence is influenced by biological communication, energy exchange, microbial activity, mycelium pathways and the interaction between plant roots and soil organisms.

The science behind SSI draws on areas such as quantum biology, ADP / ATP energy conversion, biological signalling, quorum sensing, biofilm behaviour and the rhizosphere as the primary interaction zone between plant and soil life.

The science is complex, but the purpose is practical: to support living soil systems so landowners, growers and regional partners can build stronger, more resilient and more productive country over time.

Science-backed, but practical in purpose

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Understanding SSI Technology

SSI Technology is built on advanced biological science and practical soil system knowledge. These questions explain the key principles behind soil intelligence, biological activation and the role SSI plays in supporting living soil performance.

1. What is SSI Technology?

SSI Technology is a soil intelligence activator designed to support the living biological systems beneath the plant. It works around the rhizosphere — the root-zone where plant roots, soil biology, bacteria, fungi and biological signalling interact.

2. Why is the rhizosphere so important?

The rhizosphere is the primary interaction zone between plant roots and soil life. It is where microbial activity, nutrient cycling, biological signalling and plant response are most active.

3. Why are bacteria important in soil?

Bacteria are part of the living biological community that helps soil function. In the SSI framework, beneficial bacteria are important because they influence biological activity, communication pathways, soil response and the plant root environment.

4. What is biological activation?

Biological activation means supporting the conditions that allow beneficial soil biology to become active, organised and functional. SSI Technology is designed to help biological species respond positively within the soil environment rather than simply adding another input.

5. What does quantum biology have to do with SSI Technology?

Quantum biology helps explain how energy exchange, biological signalling and molecular interaction can influence living systems, including soil biology.

SSI Technology is not claiming soil is “magic”. It is recognising that living soil systems operate through complex biological and energy-based interactions that conventional input-based agriculture often does not fully address.

6. What is ADP (Adenosine Diphosphate) and ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)?

ADP and ATP relate to biological energy transfer. SSI uses this language because living soil systems require energy conditions that allow biology to function, communicate and support plant response.

7. Why are biological products and bio-stimulants used together?

They support both the biological organisms in the soil and the conditions that help those organisms perform. In simple terms, the biology matters — but so does the environment that allows that biology to activate, communicate and function.

8. Is SSI Technology proven or still in R&D?

SSI Technology is not early-stage R&D. It is an applied biological technology platform developed for practical agricultural use and already distributed across markets including Australia, New Zealand and China.

The technology is designed to support real-world soil activation, biological communication and long-term land performance — helping landowners and growers work with the living systems beneath the plant.
